# What are the hidden gems and secret spots in South Wales?

## Quick Answer

South Wales is packed with off-the-beaten-path adventures! Skip the crowds at Pen y Fan and head to the lesser-known peaks of the Brecon Beacons National Park like Fan Hir for stunning views. Explore the secluded coves of the Gower Peninsula, such as Pwll Du Bay, accessible only by foot, or kayak along the dramatic coastline of the Vale of Glamorgan, discovering hidden waterfalls and sea caves.

## Uncover the Waterfall Country's Secrets

Waterfall Country, in the Brecon Beacons, is famed for its cascades, but venture beyond the popular Four Falls Trail to discover truly hidden gems. Sgwd Gwladus (Lady Falls) near Pontneddfechan is a stunning curtain waterfall where you can swim in the plunge pool. For a longer hike, explore the trails around Ystradfellte, where you'll find fewer crowds and equally impressive falls like Sgwd yr Eira, which you can walk behind. Consider booking a canyoning experience with a local operator like Adventure Britain (from £55 per person) to explore the waterfalls from a unique perspective. Be aware that some trails can be slippery, especially after rain, so wear appropriate footwear.

## Coastal Escapes on the Glamorgan Heritage Coast

While the Gower Peninsula gets a lot of attention, the Glamorgan Heritage Coast offers equally stunning scenery with fewer visitors. Monknash Beach, near Llantwit Major, is a secluded, rocky beach backed by dramatic cliffs – perfect for fossil hunting and rock pooling. Further west, Nash Point boasts impressive cliffs, a historic lighthouse, and stunning views across the Bristol Channel. You can walk sections of the Wales Coast Path here, but be mindful of tide times, as some sections become impassable at high tide. For a unique experience, try coasteering with a local company like Celtic Quest Coasteering (from £45 per person) and explore the coastline from the sea.

## Practical Tips

- Park responsibly in Waterfall Country. Popular spots fill up quickly, especially on weekends, so arrive early or consider using public transport.
- Check tide times before visiting coastal areas. Some beaches are completely submerged at high tide, and access routes can be cut off.
- Download offline maps. Mobile phone signal can be unreliable in remote areas of South Wales, so download maps and trail guides before you set off.
- Pack for all weather. Conditions in the Brecon Beacons and along the coast can change rapidly, so be prepared for rain, wind, and sun.
