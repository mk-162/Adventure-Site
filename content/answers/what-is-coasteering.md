# What is Coasteering?

## Quick Answer

Coasteering is an adventurous activity that involves traversing a rocky coastline without the aid of boats or boards. Typically, you'll be swimming, scrambling, climbing, and jumping your way along the intertidal zone. It's a thrilling way to explore the coast, get some exercise, and experience the marine environment up close.

## What to Expect on a Coasteering Adventure

Coasteering combines several activities into one exhilarating experience. You'll start by donning appropriate safety gear, including a wetsuit, buoyancy aid, and helmet. A qualified guide will lead you along a pre-planned route, navigating the coastline's natural features. Expect to:

*   **Swim:** Sections of the route involve swimming across inlets and bays.
*   **Scramble:** Clambering over rocks and boulders is a key element.
*   **Climb:** Short, manageable rock climbs are often incorporated.
*   **Jump:** Optional jumps from varying heights into the sea provide an adrenaline rush.
*   **Explore:** Discover hidden caves, gullies, and unique rock formations.
*   **Learn:** Gain insights into the local marine environment from your guide.

## Where Can You Go Coasteering in Wales?

Wales is arguably the UK's coasteering capital, offering diverse and dramatic coastlines perfect for this activity. Pembrokeshire is particularly well-known, boasting stunning cliffs, clear waters, and abundant wildlife. However, coasteering is also available in other coastal areas. Here's a breakdown by region:

*   **Pembrokeshire:** The premier coasteering destination, with numerous operators like TYF Adventure, Preseli Venture, Activity Pembrokeshire, Tenby Adventure, MUUK Adventures, and Outer Reef Surf School offering trips for all abilities. Expect rugged cliffs, turquoise waters, and potential wildlife encounters (seals, seabirds).
*   **Snowdonia:** While better known for its mountains, Snowdonia also offers coasteering experiences. You may need to travel to the coast.

## Tips for Your First Coasteering Trip

*   **Book with a Reputable Operator:** Safety is paramount. Choose an operator with experienced guides and proper equipment.
*   **Listen to Your Guide:** Pay attention to safety briefings and follow instructions carefully.
*   **Be Honest About Your Abilities:** Coasteering can be physically demanding. Choose a route appropriate for your fitness level.
*   **Wear Appropriate Clothing:** Wetsuits are typically provided, but bring swimwear to wear underneath.
*   **Don't Be Afraid to Ask Questions:** If you're unsure about anything, ask your guide.
*   **Embrace the Challenge:** Coasteering can be challenging, but it's also incredibly rewarding.
