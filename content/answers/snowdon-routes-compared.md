# Which Snowdon Route Should I Take?

## Quick Answer

The best Snowdon route for you depends on your experience, fitness level, and preferred difficulty. For beginners or those seeking a gentler climb, the Llanberis Path is the longest but least challenging. Experienced hikers and scramblers looking for adventure should consider Crib Goch, but be aware of its exposed knife-edge ridge.

## Snowdon Routes Compared

Snowdon offers a variety of routes to the summit, each with its own character and challenges. Hereâ€™s a breakdown of some popular options:

*   **Llanberis Path:** The longest and most gradual route, suitable for beginners and families. It follows a well-maintained path with steady inclines.
*   **Snowdon Ranger Path:** A slightly shorter and quieter alternative to the Llanberis Path, offering scenic views. The initial section is steep, but it becomes more moderate.
*   **Miner's Track:** A popular route with a relatively easy start, passing by the picturesque Llyn Llydaw and Glaslyn lakes. The final ascent is a bit steeper.
*   **Watkin Path:** The most challenging of the standard routes, with a significant ascent and varied terrain, including a waterfall section. Offers a sense of remoteness.
*   **Rhyd-Ddu Path:** A quieter and more scenic route than the Llanberis and Miner's Paths. It offers stunning views and a moderate level of difficulty.
*   **Crib Goch:** Only suitable for experienced scramblers. This is a grade 1 scramble and involves exposed knife-edge ridges and requires a head for heights. It's best tackled in good weather conditions. Consider a guided walk with MountainXperience or Snowdonia Mountain Guides if you're unsure.

**Factors to consider:**

*   **Fitness Level:** Be honest about your fitness level. Choose a route that aligns with your capabilities.
*   **Experience:** If you're new to hiking, opt for an easier route like the Llanberis Path.
*   **Weather:** Check the weather forecast before you go. Some routes, like Crib Goch, are dangerous in poor weather conditions.
*   **Time of Year:** Conditions on Snowdon can vary greatly depending on the time of year. Winter ascents require specialist equipment and experience.

## Tips for a Safe and Enjoyable Hike

*   **Check the weather:** Mountain weather can change rapidly. Be prepared for all conditions.
*   **Wear appropriate clothing:** Layers are essential. Waterproof and windproof outer layers are a must.
*   **Wear sturdy footwear:** Hiking boots with good ankle support are recommended.
*   **Carry plenty of water and food:** Stay hydrated and energized throughout your hike.
*   **Bring a map and compass (or GPS):** Familiarize yourself with the route before you go.
*   **Tell someone where you're going:** Let someone know your planned route and estimated return time.
*   **Be aware of your surroundings:** Watch out for hazards such as loose rocks and steep drop-offs.
