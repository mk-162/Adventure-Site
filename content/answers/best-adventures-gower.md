# What are the best adventure activities in Gower Peninsula?

## Quick Answer

Gower Peninsula offers a range of thrilling adventures, from surfing the waves at Llangennith and exploring the dramatic coastline by coasteering, to hiking the iconic Wales Coast Path. For a unique experience, try paragliding over Rhossili Bay or rock climbing at Three Cliffs Bay.

## Surfing and Watersports

Llangennith is renowned as one of Wales' best surfing beaches, drawing surfers of all levels. Several surf schools operate here, like PJ's Surf School, offering lessons and board rentals from around £35 for a two-hour session. Caswell Bay is another popular spot, particularly for beginners, with gentler waves and surf schools like Surf GSD. For those seeking a different kind of water adventure, Gower Coast Adventures offers exhilarating coasteering experiences along the rugged coastline, exploring hidden coves and jumping off cliffs, with prices starting around £50 per person. Kayaking and paddleboarding are also popular, with rentals available at Oxwich Bay and Three Cliffs Bay.

## Hiking and Climbing

The Gower Peninsula boasts stunning coastal trails, with the Wales Coast Path offering breathtaking views. A particularly rewarding hike is the Rhossili Down circular walk, taking you to the top of the iconic Rhossili Down for panoramic vistas of Rhossili Bay and Worm's Head. For experienced hikers, the challenging but rewarding walk to Three Cliffs Bay is a must. Rock climbing enthusiasts will find plenty of opportunities at Three Cliffs Bay, known for its limestone cliffs, with climbs suitable for various skill levels. Several local companies offer guided climbing sessions, ensuring safety and providing expert instruction.

## Practical Tips

- Check the tide times before heading to beaches like Rhossili or Three Cliffs, as access can be limited at high tide.
- Parking at popular spots like Rhossili and Three Cliffs can fill up quickly, especially during peak season. Arrive early or consider using public transport.
- Book surf lessons and coasteering adventures in advance, particularly during the summer months, to avoid disappointment.
- Be prepared for changeable weather, even in summer. Pack layers, waterproofs, and sunscreen.
