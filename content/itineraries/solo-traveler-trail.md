# Solo Traveler Trail

This itinerary is designed for the independent explorer craving a taste of the Brecon Beacons' raw beauty and adrenaline-pumping activities. Get ready to challenge yourself, meet fellow adventurers, and create unforgettable memories while navigating stunning landscapes and thrilling experiences. Expect a mix of physical exertion, stunning views, and the satisfaction of conquering the trails (and maybe a fear or two!).

## Day 1: Mountain High

### Morning
**Activity:** Mountain Biking (Full day)
Hit the trails! Explore the Brecon Beacons on two wheels. A full day of mountain biking will get your blood pumping and introduce you to the diverse terrain. There are beginner-friendly routes as well as more challenging sections, so choose your adventure.

**Travel:** 15 min drive to bike hire location.

### Afternoon
**Activity:** Continued Mountain Biking
Explore the trails! Pack lunch and find a scenic spot.

### Evening
**Stay:** Hostel
A great budget-friendly option, and likely to meet other travelers.

**Dinner suggestion:** Local pub - hearty pub grub and a pint after a day on the bike.

---

## Day 2: Water Warrior

### Morning
**Activity:** Canyoning (Half-Full day)
Embrace the wild! Canyoning is the ultimate adventure sport, combining scrambling, swimming, and jumping through stunning natural gorges. Prepare to get wet, push your limits, and witness breathtaking scenery.

**Travel:** 5 min walk to changing area.

### Afternoon
**Activity:** Relax
Relax and enjoy a hot drink after Canyoning, visit local shop or take some pictures of the area.

### Evening
**Stay:** Bunkhouse
A good alternative to a hostel, with self-catering facilities for a cheap meal.

**Dinner suggestion:** Cook at the bunkhouse - save some money and enjoy the social aspect.

---

## Day 3: Deep Dive

### Morning
**Activity:** Caving (Half day)
Delve into the darkness and explore the hidden world beneath the Brecon Beacons. Caving offers a unique perspective and a thrilling challenge as you navigate underground passages and chambers.

**Travel:** 20 min drive to caving location.

### Afternoon
**Activity:** Gorge Walking (2-3 hours)
Time for another water adventure! Gorge walking is a milder alternative to canyoning, but still a fun way to get active and see the Brecon Beacons' rivers and streams.

### Evening
**Stay:** Campsite
Get back to nature and enjoy an evening under the stars.

**Dinner suggestion:** Cook on campsite - campfire cuisine!

---

## Day 4: Heights and Hikes

### Morning
**Activity:** Rock Climbing (Half day)
Reach new heights, with rock climbing. Enjoy amazing views and challenge yourself.

**Travel:** 10 min drive to the rock climbing spot.

### Afternoon
**Activity:** Hiking
Enjoy a hike through one of the Beacons' peaks.
Explore the area.

### Evening
**Stay:** B&B
Treat yourself to some home comforts after a few days of adventurous activities.

**Dinner suggestion:** The Felin Fach Griffin - award-winning gastropub with local ales.

## Tips

- Book activities in advance, especially during peak season, to avoid disappointment.
- Pack for all types of weather – layers are key! Even in summer, the mountains can be unpredictable.
- The Brecon Beacons are best explored with a good map and compass (and the ability to use them!). Don't rely solely on your phone's GPS, as signal can be patchy.
