# What are the best Kayaking tips for beginners?

## Quick Answer

Start with a guided tour from a reputable operator like TYF Adventure in St Davids. They’ll teach you the basics of paddling, safety, and navigation in calm waters. Always check the weather forecast and tide times before heading out, and wear appropriate clothing, including a buoyancy aid.

## Getting Started with Kayaking in Wales

Wales offers incredible kayaking opportunities, but starting safely is key. For sea kayaking, consider a beginner session with Celtic Quest Coasteering in Pembrokeshire. They offer half-day trips from £45, introducing you to paddling techniques and coastal exploration. You'll learn how to handle your kayak in the swell and navigate around stunning rock formations like The Green Bridge of Wales. River kayaking is also popular; try a gentle paddle on the River Wye with Wye Valley Canoes, with rentals starting from around £30 per day. They provide maps and guidance for a self-guided tour, perfect for building confidence on moving water.

Remember that Welsh weather can change rapidly. Even on a sunny day, conditions at sea can become challenging quickly. Always check the Marine Forecast and be prepared to turn back if necessary. Inland, river levels can rise after heavy rain, making kayaking unsuitable for beginners.

## Choosing the Right Kayak and Location

Different kayaks and locations suit different skill levels. A wide, stable kayak is best for beginners, especially for sea kayaking. Sit-on-top kayaks are easy to get in and out of, making them a good choice if you're nervous about capsizing. For river kayaking, a shorter kayak is more maneuverable.

## Practical Tips

- Always wear a properly fitted buoyancy aid – it could save your life. Check that it's not too tight to restrict breathing, but snug enough that it won't slip off in the water.
- Learn how to do a "wet exit" – practice capsizing in calm, shallow water so you know how to get out of your kayak if it flips.
- Book your kayaking trip in advance, especially during peak season (June-August) and school holidays. Parking at popular spots like Llyn Padarn can fill up quickly, so arrive early.
- Pack a dry bag with essentials like sunscreen, water, snacks, a waterproof phone case, and a small first-aid kit. Even on a short trip, being prepared makes a big difference.
