**Who it's for:** Blue-grade riders ready to progress, returning riders rebuilding fitness and nerve, anyone who wants red-grade bragging rights without red-grade terror.

**What to expect:** The first half lulls you into confidence with flowing singletrack and manageable features. **Honest warning:** This is still a red trail. You will encounter loose surfaces, off-camber sections, and features that require commitment. If the MinorTaur felt challenging, give yourself more time before tackling Cyflym Coch.

**Who it's for:** Experienced riders short on time, anyone wanting the best bits without the full Beast commitment, riders building toward the big one.

**Honest warning:** Concentrated quality means concentrated difficulty. There's no easy way through MBR â€” every kilometre demands your attention. This isn't a trail for tired riders.

|---|---|
| **Distance** | 38.2 km |

| **Surface** | Technical singletrack |

This is it. The Beast. Coed y Brenin's biggest challenge and one of the UK's most demanding trail centre routes. 38km of everything mountain biking can throw at you: fast open sections, tight technical bits, flowing singletrack, bone-shaking rocky terrain. If you complete The Beast, you've earned something.

**Who it's for:** Experienced, fit riders seeking a genuine challenge. Anyone who's ridden the other blacks and wants more. Riders training for endurance events who need trail time.

**What to expect:** 1,000m of climbing over 38km is no joke. The trail includes sections from other routes but links them with additional challenges. You'll encounter everything: flow, tech, speed, grind, and several moments where you question your life choices. The satisfaction at the end is proportional to the suffering along the way.

**Honest warning:** The Beast breaks people. Not through any single feature, but through relentless accumulation. At 30km, when your legs are screaming and concentration is wavering, you'll still have technical terrain ahead. Under-prepared riders end up walking significant sections, which destroys both the experience and the timing.

**Requirements:** 
- Proper fitness (you should be comfortable with 3+ hour rides)
- Confident black-grade skills
- Full hydration and nutrition strategy
- Mechanical competence (you're a long way from help if something breaks)
- The right mindset (this is an endurance event, not a casual ride)

**Local tip:** Don't attempt The Beast on your first visit to Coed y Brenin. Ride the other blacks first. Understand the terrain. Then come back for The Beast when you know what you're getting into.

## When to Visit

**Best time:** Spring and autumn. Warm enough to enjoy, quiet enough to park, trails in prime condition.

**Summer:** Trails are great but parking becomes a battleground. Weekdays are better.

**Winter:** Trails drain well, but short days limit riding time. CafÃ© becomes even more important.

**After rain:** Coed y Brenin drains better than almost anywhere in Wales. You can ride here when other trails are swamps.
