# When should I avoid visiting Gower Peninsula?

## Quick Answer

Avoid Gower during peak summer holidays (late July to August) and bank holiday weekends if you dislike crowds, as popular spots like Rhossili Bay and Three Cliffs Bay become extremely busy. Parking can be a nightmare, and trails become congested. For a quieter experience, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons (May-June or September-October).

## Avoiding the Crowds

Gower's beauty is no secret, and during peak times, its most famous locations can feel more like a theme park than a tranquil escape. Rhossili Bay, regularly voted one of the UK's best beaches, sees a huge influx of visitors in July and August. Similarly, Three Cliffs Bay, known for its dramatic cliffs and challenging terrain, becomes crowded, especially around the stepping stones across the river. This can make navigating the area, particularly with young children, quite stressful.

Consider less well-known spots like Pwll Du Bay, a secluded pebble beach accessible via a scenic coastal path from Southgate. It's a bit of a walk, which deters some visitors, or Broughton Bay, which offers a vast expanse of sand even at high tide. Alternatively, explore the northern coast of Gower, which tends to be quieter than the south.

## Weather Considerations

While Gower enjoys a relatively mild climate, the weather can be unpredictable, especially during the winter months (November to February). Coastal paths can become muddy and slippery, and strong winds are common. Some outdoor activities, such as kayaking or coasteering with providers like Gower Adventures, may be cancelled due to unsafe sea conditions. Check the forecast before you go, and be prepared for all types of weather, even in summer.

## Practical Tips

- Book accommodation and activities well in advance, especially if you're travelling during peak season. Many campsites and guesthouses fill up months ahead.
- Consider using the Park & Ride service operating from Swansea during the summer to access the Gower, avoiding parking hassles in the villages.
- Check tide times before visiting beaches like Rhossili or Three Cliffs, as access may be restricted at high tide.
- Be aware that some coastal paths can be challenging, with steep drops and uneven surfaces. Wear appropriate footwear and take care, especially in wet weather.
