About Coasteering

What to Expect expand_more
Coasteering involves traversing a rocky coastline on foot or by swimming, without the aid of boats, surfboards or other craft. It includes cliff jumping, swimming, scrambling and climbing. Snowdonia offers some of the best rugged coastlines for this adventure.
Requirements expand_more
Generally, you need to be confident in water. Most operators provide wetsuits, buoyancy aids, and helmets. Minimum age usually starts from 8 years old for family sessions, but check individual listings.

Why Choose Coasteering in Snowdonia?

Snowdonia offers some of the most dramatic coastlines in the UK. Combining cliffs, waves, and wildlife, coasteering here is a unique way to experience the natural environment up close. Unlike simply walking the coastal path, you'll be immersed in the elements.

Whether you are looking for heart-pounding jumps or a scenic swim into hidden sea caves, our verified operators provide safe and exhilarating experiences tailored to all skill levels. All equipment, including wetsuits, buoyancy aids, and helmets, is typically provided.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be a strong swimmer? expand_more

Not necessarily. While water confidence helps, the buoyancy aids will keep you afloat. Always check the difficulty rating of the specific activity.

What should I bring? expand_more

You will need a swimsuit to wear under the wetsuit, a towel, and sturdy footwear (like old trainers) that you don't mind getting wet.

Is it safe for children? expand_more

Yes, many operators offer family-specific trips with age limits usually starting around 8 years old.
