# When should I avoid visiting South Wales?

## Quick Answer

Avoid South Wales during peak school holidays (late July/August and around Christmas) if you dislike crowds; popular spots like Pen y Fan and the Gower beaches become extremely busy. Also, be aware that some adventure activities, especially those involving water like canyoning with companies such as 'Adventures Activity South Wales' in Pontneddfechan, are heavily weather-dependent and may be cancelled during periods of prolonged heavy rain or flooding.

## Dodging the Crowds: Peak Season Realities

South Wales bursts into life during the summer holidays, but this popularity comes at a cost. The Brecon Beacons National Park, especially around Pen y Fan, can feel more like a festival than a mountain walk in late July and August. Parking becomes a nightmare, trails are congested, and the overall experience is diminished. Similarly, the Gower Peninsula's stunning beaches like Rhossili Bay and Three Cliffs Bay are packed with families, making it challenging to find a quiet spot or even a parking space. Expect to pay upwards of Â£10 for parking at popular beach locations during peak times. If you prefer solitude and a more authentic experience, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons (May-June or September-October) when the weather is still pleasant, but the crowds have thinned out.

Consider that major events, like the Welsh Open golf tournament at Celtic Manor in Newport, can also significantly impact accommodation availability and prices in the surrounding areas. Check event calendars before booking your trip, especially if you're on a budget.

## Weather Woes and Seasonal Closures

While South Wales enjoys a relatively mild climate, it's also known for its unpredictable weather. Winter months (November-February) can bring heavy rain, strong winds, and even occasional snow, particularly in the Brecon Beacons. This can make hiking challenging and some outdoor activities dangerous. Several smaller campsites and B&Bs close during the off-season. Coastal activities like coasteering with TYF Adventure near St Davids become less appealing (and potentially unsafe) due to rough seas and cold water temperatures. Always check the weather forecast before heading out and be prepared to change your plans if necessary.

## Practical Tips

- Book accommodation and activities well in advance, especially if travelling during peak season.
- Check the Brecon Beacons National Park website for parking updates and trail closures.
- Download a reliable weather app and be prepared for all conditions, even in summer.
- Consider visiting less well-known areas like the Vale of Glamorgan for a quieter experience.
