# What is the best time of year for Hiking in Wales?

## Quick Answer

The best time for hiking in Wales is generally late spring to early autumn (May-September) for the most favorable weather. During these months, you'll find milder temperatures and longer daylight hours, perfect for exploring trails in Eryri (Snowdonia), the Brecon Beacons, or along the Pembrokeshire Coast Path. Be prepared for changeable conditions though, as Wales is known for its unpredictable weather, even in summer.

## Spring and Summer Hiking in Wales

Spring (May-June) brings blooming wildflowers and a burst of color to the Welsh landscape. This is a great time to tackle trails like the ascent of Yr Wyddfa (Snowdon) via the Llanberis Path or the more challenging Crib Goch route. Many local guiding companies, such as RAW Adventures based in Llanberis, offer guided Snowdon hikes starting from around £50 per person. Summer (July-August) offers the warmest temperatures, making it ideal for coastal hikes along the Pembrokeshire Coast Path or exploring the Gower Peninsula. However, summer also brings more crowds, especially to popular spots like Pen y Fan in the Brecon Beacons.

## Autumn Hiking in Wales

Autumn (September-October) can be a truly magical time for hiking in Wales. The hillsides transform into a tapestry of reds, oranges, and browns, offering stunning views. The Rhondda Valley, with its industrial heritage trails, looks particularly striking in the autumn light. Be aware that daylight hours are shorter, and the weather can become more unpredictable with increased rainfall and potential for strong winds, especially at higher elevations. Always check the mountain weather forecast before heading out.

## Practical Tips

- Keep an eye on the Mountain Weather Information Service (MWIS) forecast – it's essential for planning any hike in Wales, regardless of the time of year.
- Park responsibly! In popular areas like Snowdonia, parking fills up quickly, especially during peak season. Consider using the Snowdon Sherpa bus service to access trailheads.
- Book accommodation and activities in advance, particularly if you're visiting during school holidays. Places like Betws-y-Coed and Abergavenny get very busy.
- Pack for all eventualities. Even on a sunny day, the weather in the mountains can change rapidly. Waterproofs, warm layers, and a map and compass (and the skills to use them!) are essential.
