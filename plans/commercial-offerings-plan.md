# Adventure Wales Commercial Offerings Plan

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Prepared for:** Adventure Wales Business Owner

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Adventure Wales is positioned to become Wales's premier adventure tourism directory, currently featuring 46 businesses, 70 accommodation listings, 128 journal articles, and 54 itineraries. This plan outlines nine distinct revenue streams with pricing benchmarks based on comprehensive competitor analysis.

### Revenue Potential Overview

Based on current assets and industry benchmarks:

- **Immediate Revenue Opportunity:** £50,000-£150,000 annually (Year 1)
- **12-Month Growth Target:** £200,000-£400,000 annually (Year 2)
- **Mature Revenue Target:** £500,000+ annually (Year 3+)

### Key Recommendations

1. **Launch with freemium operator listings** (free stub → £49/mo claimed → £149/mo premium)
2. **Integrate booking commissions** as primary revenue driver (8-12% commission target)
3. **Implement Booking.com affiliate** for quick accommodation wins (4% commission)
4. **Phase in lead generation** once traffic scales (£20-£80 per qualified lead)
5. **Develop premium content** partnerships with operators (£500-£2,000/mo)

### 2.3 Booking System Integration & Commissions

**Overview:** The highest-potential revenue stream. Enable direct bookings through the site and earn commission. This requires booking widget integration.

#### Commission Structure

| Booking Type | Commission Rate | Industry Benchmark |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Activity/Tour Bookings** | 8-12% | Viator: 20-30%<br>GetYourGuide: 20-30%<br>Much Better Adventures: 8%<br>Beyonk: 4% |
| **Accommodation** | 4-6% | Booking.com: 15% to property (4% to affiliate)<br>Hotels.com: Similar |
| **Gear Rental** | 10-15% | Varies widely, higher margin |
| **Transport/Transfers** | 5-8% | Lower margin, volume play |

#### Recommended Model

**Adventure Wales Booking Commission: 10%**
- Competitive vs 20-30% charged by GetYourGuide/Viator
- Higher than Much Better Adventures (8%) but includes marketing/visibility
- Operators keep 90% (vs 70-80% on major OTAs)
- Position as "fair, Wales-focused alternative to big platforms"

#### Booking Systems to Integrate

Research shows UK adventure operators commonly use:

| Platform | Commission Model | Integration Difficulty |
|----------|------------------|----------------------|
| **FareHarbor** | 6% booking fee (no monthly) | Easy - API available |
| **Bookwhen** | £10-£75/mo subscription | Medium - API available |
| **Rezdy** | 3% + £49-£199/mo | Medium - API available |
| **Beyonk** | 4% booking fee | Easy - Widget + API |
| **Custom/Manual** | N/A | Phone/email enquiries only |

**Recommendation:** Start with **FareHarbor** and **Beyonk** integration (most common among UK operators), then expand to Rezdy/Bookwhen.

#### Revenue Projections

**Assumptions:**
- Average booking value: £150 per person
- Conversion rate: 1-3% of site visitors book through platform
- Traffic growth: 10,000 → 50,000 → 150,000 visitors/month over 3 years

| Year | Monthly Visitors | Bookings/Month | Avg Value | Commission (10%) | Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue |
|------|------------------|----------------|-----------|------------------|-----------------|----------------|

| **Year 3** | 150,000 | 750 | £200 | £15,000 | £450,000 | £5,400,000 |

*Note: These are optimistic projections. Real-world Year 1 likely £30,000-£80,000; Year 2: £150,000-£400,000*

#### Implementation Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3):** Pilot Program
- Partner with 5-10 operators using FareHarbor/Beyonk
- Zero commission for first 50 bookings (prove value)
- Build booking widget infrastructure
- Test conversion funnel

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6):** Scale
- Launch 10% commission structure
- Onboard 20+ operators
- Add Rezdy integration
- Implement booking analytics dashboard

**Phase 3 (Months 7-12):** Optimize
- A/B test booking flows

### 2.5 Lead Generation

**Overview:** Charge operators for qualified enquiries. The site already has "Enquire All Vendors" functionality on itinerary pages.

#### Pricing Models

**Model A: Pay-Per-Lead**
- Charge per qualified enquiry sent to operator
- **Pricing:** £20-£80 per lead (depending on activity value)
  - Coasteering/kayaking day trip: £20-£30
  - Multi-day expedition: £50-£80
  - Accommodation booking: £15-£25
  - Gear rental: £10-£20

**Model B: Monthly Lead Subscription**
- Flat monthly fee for unlimited leads in category
- **Pricing:** £150-£500/month
- Good for high-volume operators

**Model C: Lead Guarantee**
- "10 qualified leads or money back"
- **Pricing:** £400-£800/month
- Premium positioning

#### UK Lead Generation Benchmarks

- **General B2B leads:** £50-£500/lead
- **High-value B2B:** £300-£1,000/lead
- **Adventure tourism lead value:** £20-£150 (depending on booking value)
- **Conversion rate (lead → booking):** 10-30% industry standard

#### Lead Quality Criteria

To justify pricing, define "qualified lead":
- ✅ Contact details provided (name, email, phone)
- ✅ Specific activity/date enquiry
- ✅ Party size indicated
- ✅ Within operator's service area
- ✅ Verified email (not spam)

#### Revenue Projections

**Assumptions:**
- Enquiry forms on all 54 itineraries + 46 business pages
- Conversion rate: 0.5-2% of visitors submit enquiry
- Average leads per month: 50 → 300 → 1,000 (Year 1 → 2 → 3)

| Year | Leads/Month | Avg Price/Lead | Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue |
|------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Year 1** | 50 | £25 | £1,250 | £15,000 |
| **Year 2** | 300 | £35 | £10,500 | £126,000 |
| **Year 3** | 1,000 | £40 | £40,000 | £480,000 |

#### Implementation Strategy

- **Free trial:** First 90 days, send leads free to prove value
- **Analytics dashboard:** Show operators lead quality, conversion rates
- **Lead scoring:** Tag leads as "hot/warm/cold" based on detail/urgency
- **CRM integration:** Connect with operator's booking systems where possible
- **Geo-targeting:** Prioritize leads to nearest operators

### 2.6 Accommodation Revenue (Affiliate + Direct)

**Overview:** 70 accommodation listings is a strong foundation. Monetize through affiliate commissions and direct partnerships.

#### Booking.com Affiliate Program

**Commission Structure:**
- **Accommodation bookings:** 4% commission (on Booking.com's 15% fee to property)
- **Car rentals:** 6% commission
- **Flights:** £2/€2 per booking
- **Minimum payout:** €100

**How it works:**
1. Deep-link each accommodation listing to Booking.com
2. User clicks through from Adventure Wales
3. Booking.com pays 4% commission on completed stays
4. 24-hour cookie window

## 7. Risks & Considerations

### 7.1 Operator Adoption Risks

**Risk:** Operators reluctant to pay for listings when free option exists.

**Mitigation:**
- Clear value demonstration: "Premium listings get 5x more enquiries"
- Launch discount: 50% off for first 12 months
- Case studies: Feature success stories from early adopters
- Free trial: 30-day money-back guarantee

**Risk:** Operators already on TripAdvisor/GetYourGuide don't see value in another platform.

**Mitigation:**
- Position as complementary, not replacement: "Wales-focused audience"
- Lower commission rates (10% vs 20-30%)
- Better support + local focus
- Itinerary integration = unique value add

### 7.2 Booking Integration Challenges

**Risk:** Technical complexity of integrating multiple booking systems.

**Mitigation:**
- Start with 1-2 systems (FareHarbor, Beyonk) used by most operators
- Use existing widgets/APIs (don't build from scratch)
- Partner with booking platform providers for implementation support

**Risk:** Operators hesitant to add another commission layer.

**Mitigation:**
- Offer zero-commission pilot period (prove ROI)
- Position as "marketing cost" not "extra commission"
- Show booking volume growth compensates for commission cost

### 7.3 Traffic & SEO Risks

**Risk:** Insufficient traffic to drive bookings/leads at scale.

**Mitigation:**
- Content marketing: 128 articles is a strong foundation — keep publishing
- SEO optimization: Target long-tail keywords ("coasteering Pembrokeshire," "mountain biking Snowdonia")
- Social media: Build Instagram/Facebook following
- Partnerships: Collaborate with Visit Wales, regional tourism boards
- Paid acquisition: Google Ads, Meta Ads once unit economics proven

**Risk:** Google algorithm changes impact rankings.

**Mitigation:**
- Diversify traffic sources (social, email, partnerships, direct)
- Build email list (own audience)
- Focus on quality content + user experience (algorithm-resistant)

### 7.4 Competitive Risks

**Risk:** TripAdvisor or GetYourGuide expands Wales-specific focus.

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage: Build operator relationships now
- Better service: Local support, lower commissions, operator-first approach
- Niche focus: They're global platforms; we're Wales experts

**Risk:** Another Wales-specific adventure directory launches.

**Mitigation:**
- Proprietary content: 128 articles, 54 itineraries = barrier to entry
- Operator lock-in: Premium subscribers unlikely to switch
- Brand building: "Adventure Wales" name + domain is strong

### 7.5 Revenue Dependency Risks

**Risk:** Over-reliance on booking commissions (50%+ of revenue).

**Mitigation:**
- Diversify revenue streams (9 identified)
- Build recurring revenue (subscriptions, not just transactions)
- Balance transactional (bookings) with subscription (listings, leads)

**Risk:** Seasonality of adventure tourism (summer-heavy).

**Mitigation:**
- Promote winter activities (coasteering, indoor climbing, winter skills)
- Annual billing for operator listings (smooth revenue)
- Off-season content campaigns ("Plan Your Summer Adventure")

### 7.6 Legal & Compliance Considerations

**Risk:** Consumer protection regulations for bookings (Package Travel Regulations).

**Mitigation:**
- Legal review of booking terms & conditions
- Consider ATOL/ABTA bonding if offering packaged itineraries
- Clear disclaimers: Adventure Wales is marketplace, not tour operator

**Risk:** Data protection (GDPR) for lead generation.

**Mitigation:**
- Explicit opt-in for marketing communications
- Clear privacy policy
- Data processing agreements with operators receiving leads

**Risk:** Advertising standards (ASA) for sponsored content.

**Mitigation:**
- Clear labeling: "Sponsored," "Partner Content," "Advertisement"
- Disclosure requirements met for affiliate links
