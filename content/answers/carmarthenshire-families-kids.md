# Is Carmarthenshire good for families with children?

## Quick Answer

Absolutely! Carmarthenshire offers a diverse range of family-friendly activities, from sandy beaches like Pendine Sands where kids can run wild, to fascinating historical sites like Kidwelly Castle. With options for all ages and abilities, you'll find plenty to keep everyone entertained.

## Beach Days and Coastal Adventures

Carmarthenshire's coastline is a real draw for families. Pendine Sands is famous for its long, flat beach – perfect for building sandcastles, kite flying, and even attempting land speed records (though maybe not with the family car!). During the summer, you can often find events and activities happening right on the beach. For a quieter experience, head to Llansteffan Beach, overlooked by the imposing Llansteffan Castle. The castle ruins are great for exploring, and the beach is ideal for paddling and picnicking. Expect to pay around £3-£5 for parking at the more popular beaches during peak season.

For a unique wildlife encounter, consider a boat trip from Dale (just over the border in Pembrokeshire, but easily accessible). Several operators offer family-friendly tours, with opportunities to spot seals, dolphins, and seabirds. Prices typically range from £20-£30 per person.

## Castles, Caves and Animal Encounters

Beyond the beaches, Carmarthenshire boasts a wealth of historical and natural attractions. Kidwelly Castle is a must-visit, with its impressive Norman architecture and fascinating history. Kids will love exploring the towers and imagining life in medieval times. Entry fees are around £7 for adults and £5 for children.

For a subterranean adventure, head to the National Showcaves Centre for Wales, just north of Carmarthenshire near Ystradgynlais. While technically outside the county, it's an easy day trip. Explore the stunning caves, see dinosaur exhibits, and let the kids burn off some energy in the playground. Family tickets cost around £50. Alternatively, for animal lovers, there's Manor Wildlife Park. Although this is just over the border into Pembrokeshire, it's a short drive from Carmarthen and offers a wide range of animals in a zoo setting.

## Practical Tips

- Check the tide times before heading to the beach, especially Pendine Sands, as the tide comes in quickly.
- Pack a picnic to save money on food and drinks, especially if you're visiting a popular attraction.
- Many of the castles and historical sites offer family tickets, which can save you money compared to buying individual tickets.
- Bring waterproofs and layers, even in summer, as the weather in Wales can be unpredictable.
